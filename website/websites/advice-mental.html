Mental Health & Well-being

Small daily habits, who to talk to, and when to seek professional help.

Look after yourself

Sleep, movement, and routine are powerful. If things feel overwhelming, speak to a trusted adult or local health services.

Reach out

Practical support and professional help

When to seek help

If you feel constantly low, have trouble sleeping, or think about harming yourself, reach out to a trusted adult and a professional. It's OK to ask for help — it's a strong step.

Immediate support

For urgent help in Romania, contact local emergency services or these resources below.

Romania resources: 116 123 (European Helpline for children and young people — check local routing), Salvation Army Romania

Professional options

Look for a school counselor, family doctor referral, or psychologists/psychiatrists listed on the regional health sites. Many services provide low-cost or sliding-scale sessions.

Romania resources: Sanatate.gov.ro (health services), Romanian Mental Health NGOs

Where to learn more

  • Charitable and social support organizations
  • Romanian health portals and directories
  • School counsellors, local family doctors and community youth centres
